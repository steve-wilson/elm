The split is a choice, not a formula

More training data tends to produce a better model. More test data gives you a more reliable estimate of how that model will actually perform. Neither is free — every example you give to one side you take from the other.

In practice, 80% train / 20% test is a common starting point. But practitioners adjust it: with very small datasets they might keep more for testing; with enormous ones the exact ratio matters less.

The harder problem: every time you use the test set to make a decision, it becomes a little less trustworthy as a benchmark.

In Level 3, you'll work with a setup that lets you make decisions freely — without touching the test set at all.
